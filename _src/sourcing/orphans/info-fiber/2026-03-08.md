## Claims Reviewed

- Getting 25–30g/day of dietary fiber is associated with meaningfully lower all-cause mortality
- Large NIH-AARP Diet and Health Study (~400,000 participants): high fiber = 22% lower all-cause mortality in men, 19% lower in women
- Pooled data from 17 prospective cohort studies: each 10g/day increase in fiber = ~10% reduction in all-cause mortality
- Fiber mechanisms: lowers LDL (binds bile acids), stabilizes blood glucose/insulin, feeds gut bacteria → SCFA production
- Average American consumes <15g/day vs recommended 25–38g/day

All claims are well-supported. The "2–5 years longer" lifespan claim from the Reddit post body is an informal interpretation; not cited to a specific study and excluded from the resource.
