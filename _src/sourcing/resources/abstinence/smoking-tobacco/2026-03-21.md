# Smoking Tobacco — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/abstinence/smoking-tobacco.md`
- **Leads**: Web search for smoking mortality, cessation benefits meta-analyses 2022–2024

## Claims Reviewed

- Life expectancy lost: confirmed — consistent 10–11 year reduction across large cohort studies worldwide
- Cessation recovery: confirmed — quitting at 35 → ~10 years recovered; at 45 → ~6 years; at 65 → 1.4–3.7 years (sex-dependent)
- All-cause mortality: confirmed — 2× mortality vs never-smokers; reduces toward never-smoker baseline over 20+ years post-cessation
- Cardiovascular: confirmed — rapid recovery; CHD risk halved within 1 year; approaches never-smoker level in 10–15 years
- Cancer: confirmed — lung cancer risk -50% at 10 years post-cessation; 14 cancer types causally linked
- Cessation success: confirmed — 5% unaided long-term success; varenicline + behavioral support >40% at 52 weeks; NRT ~doubles unaided success
- Global burden: confirmed — GBD 2021: 175M deaths and 4.3B YLLs attributable to smoking 1990–2021
