## Claims Reviewed

- To-do list writing at bedtime reduces sleep onset: confirmed — Scullin et al. 2018 (*Journal of Experimental Psychology: General*, PMC5758411), n=57, RCT with polysomnography. To-do list group fell asleep 9 min faster (15.8 vs 25.1 min), Cohen's d=0.63. More specific lists produced stronger effects (r=−0.39).
- Mechanism (cognitive offloading): confirmed as proposed mechanism; full mechanistic pathway acknowledged as still under investigation in the original paper.
- Pre-sleep worry reduction: supported by CBT-I "worry list" technique and 2024 PMC bedtime routine study (PMC11417809) which identified planning as a commonly reported sleep-onset strategy.
- Evening routine / wind-down as behavioral zeitgeber: moderate evidence; supported by sleep hygiene guidelines and CBT-I protocols but less studied as a standalone circadian mechanism.
- Morning readiness / implementation intentions: well-established in behavioral psychology literature (Gollwitzer 1999 and subsequent replications), though specific studies on next-day planning at bedtime are limited.
