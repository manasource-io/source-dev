## Claims Reviewed

The Reddit post linked to a techfixated.com article claiming autophagy begins as soon as the body gets hungry. Community comments (high-quality discussion) challenged this:

- Meaningful autophagy induction in humans appears to require 48–72h+ fasts based on current evidence
- Short daily fasting windows (16:8) provide metabolic benefits (insulin sensitivity, weight management) but the autophagy-specific evidence is weak
- Yoshinori Ohsumi won the 2016 Nobel Prize for autophagy mechanism research, but human timing thresholds remain uncertain

The original techfixated.com source was not used. The Examine.com and PMC references in the resource are credible and cover both the metabolic and autophagy aspects correctly.

## Notes

The autophagy timing question is genuinely contested — some researchers argue even short fasts upregulate autophagy markers, while others require significant ketosis before calling it "meaningful" autophagy. The current description strikes a reasonable balance. The `genetic-renewal` buff mapping is appropriate given autophagy's cellular renewal role.
