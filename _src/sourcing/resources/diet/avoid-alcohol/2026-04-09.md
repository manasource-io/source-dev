### New Studies Surfaced (2024–2026)

- **PMID 40242500** — Biases in all-cause mortality studies re: alcohol, 2025 (*Lancet Regional Health Americas*). Argues that "sick quitter" confounding and methodological flaws inflate apparent harm in observational data; calls for cause-specific approaches. Credibility: moderate. Not added as a reference — challenges methodology of existing references but does not undermine the cancer or liver evidence.
- **PMID 39133491** — Alcohol consumption and mortality in older adults with health/SES risk factors, 2024. Finds even low-risk drinking associated with higher mortality in this subgroup. Consistent with current resource framing.
- **PMID 37000449** — Updated systematic review on daily alcohol intake and all-cause mortality (JAMA Network Open, 2023). Low/moderate intake not significantly associated with all-cause mortality at population level, but higher levels show clear monotonic risk. Nuances the existing PMID 32209032.
- **Nature Scientific Reports 2024** — Mendelian randomization on alcohol and lifespan in Europeans (https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-73333-8). Uses genetic instruments to control for confounding; finds alcohol reduces lifespan, weakening the "J-curve" cardiovascular claim.

None of these required adding new references to the resource file; the existing references remain the most citable anchors. The Mendelian randomization finding is reflected in the updated "Liver and Cardiovascular Health" section.
