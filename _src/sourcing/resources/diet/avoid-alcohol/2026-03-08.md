## Claims Reviewed

Alcohol-harm evidence is exceptionally strong across multiple disease categories:

- **Cancer**: WHO classifies alcohol as a Group 1 carcinogen (definite human carcinogen). Cancers causally linked: mouth, pharynx, larynx, esophagus, liver, colorectum, breast
- **Liver**: Dose-dependent progression — steatosis → steatohepatitis → fibrosis → cirrhosis
- **Cardiovascular**: Previously thought "cardioprotective" at low doses; more recent Mendelian randomization studies challenge this — any apparent benefit may reflect confounding
- **Sleep**: Disrupts sleep architecture at any dose — reduces REM sleep, increases awakenings in second half of night
- **Cognitive**: Directly neurotoxic; even moderate use associated with smaller hippocampal volumes
- **Mental health**: Associated with depression and anxiety at population level
