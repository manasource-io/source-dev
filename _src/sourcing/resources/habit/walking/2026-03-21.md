## Claims Reviewed

**Critical issue found**: The existing reference PubMed 32239145 was about breast cancer patients and physical activity — it is completely unrelated to the resource's claim that "each additional 1,000 steps daily can significantly lower mortality risk" in a general population. This reference was marked as `removed/unrelated` in source-checks.csv and removed from the resource.

The AHA journal URL (doi/10.1161/JAHA.120.016888) returned 403 and could not be verified. Kept as `pending` in source-checks.

Replaced with two high-quality meta-analyses:

- PMC9289978: Lancet meta-analysis of 15 international cohorts on steps and all-cause mortality
- PubMed 37555441: Banach et al. European J Preventive Cardiology, 17 cohorts, 226,889 participants

The general claim about walking and mortality is extremely well-supported in the literature — the 2025 Lancet Public Health dose-response meta-analysis (57 studies, 35 cohorts) is the most comprehensive evidence to date.
