# Sunlight Exposure — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/habits/sunlight-exposure.md`
- **Leads**: Web search for morning sunlight, circadian rhythms, mood, mental health evidence 2023–2024

## Claims Reviewed

- Circadian alignment: confirmed — morning light is primary zeitgeber; phase-advances clock; 2,500 lux for 30 min sufficient to shift circadian timing
- Mood/mental health: confirmed — UK Biobank (400K+): more outdoor light → lower depression, anxiety, self-harm risk; dose-dependent; Luo 2023 confirms occupational sunlight deprivation and mental health
- Sleep quality: confirmed — morning sunlight predicts better next-night sleep (2024 daily diary study); UK Biobank: more outdoor light → longer sleep, fewer disturbances, earlier midpoint
- Serotonin: confirmed — light directly stimulates serotonin synthesis in dorsal raphe nucleus; mechanism of SAD and light therapy efficacy
- Vitamin D synthesis: confirmed — UVB converts 7-dehydrocholesterol to vitamin D3; latitude/season-dependent; 10–30 min midday sun sufficient for most people
- Indoor vs outdoor lux: confirmed — indoor 200–500 lux vs outdoor 10,000–100,000 lux — orders of magnitude difference

## Notes

- credibility `moderate`: mechanisms are very strong (photobiology is well-established); population associations are large and consistent; but the randomized trial base for most outcomes (other than SAD/light therapy) is smaller than for exercise or diet interventions
- benefitLevel 7: the circadian effect is foundational but the magnitude of harm from avoiding sunlight varies; vitamin D deficiency is separately harmful but addressable via supplementation
- overallScore = round(7 × 0.85) = 6
- Key practical insight: the lux difference between indoor and outdoor is so large that most people dramatically underestimate how light-deprived their indoor lives are
- The vitamin D latitude/season issue (no synthesis above 35°N in winter) is important practical information for users
