# Fermented Foods — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/fermented-foods.md`
- **Leads**: WebSearch — Wastyk et al. 2021 Cell study confirmed; fermented foods gut-brain axis literature 2024

## Claims Reviewed

The Cell 2021 RCT (PMID 34256014) is confirmed as the landmark study for microbiome diversity and immune marker reduction. Both claims in the resource — microbiome diversity increase and 19 inflammatory protein reduction — are accurately described. The dose-dependent effect was confirmed.

The gut-brain axis section (PMC 4367209) is valid as a foundational review of bidirectional gut-brain communication.

A 2024 Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews article (PMID 38278378) provides direct support for fermented foods and the microbiota-gut-brain axis for mental health, including kefir, kimchi, and fermented dairy. This strengthens the existing mental health section.

A 2025 Frontiers in Nutrition systematic review on Lactobacillus/Bifidobacterium in fermented foods and cognitive health adds emerging evidence for the cognitive benefits claim.
