# Cacao — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/cacao.md`
- **Leads**: PubMed search for COSMOS trial, cocoa flavanol meta-analyses; PMC11992872 (2025 systematic review)

## Claims Reviewed

The original stub correctly identified flavanols, blood pressure, brain blood flow, and theobromine as key elements. The MedicalNewsToday reference returned 404 and was removed. Claims were expanded and grounded in primary evidence:

- COSMOS trial (PubMed 35294962): 21,442-participant RCT — the largest evidence base for any dietary flavanol intervention. The 27% CVD mortality reduction is a meaningful finding even though total CVD events didn't reach significance
- Vascular function: Multiple RCTs confirm FMD improvement and pulse wave velocity reduction; the 2025 systematic review (PMC11992872) synthesizes microvascular evidence
- Insulin sensitivity: Meta-analysis of RCTs (PMC5086796) confirms improvements in insulin signaling and lipid profile
- `credibility: strong` is appropriate — there is a landmark large RCT plus multiple systematic reviews

## Notes

- The COSMOS trial used 500 mg/day flavanols — this is achievable with ~2–3 tablespoons of raw cacao powder; consumer products vary widely
- Milk chocolate and heavily processed cocoa lose most flavanols — this distinction is important for user guidance
- No existing effects.json entry to audit
