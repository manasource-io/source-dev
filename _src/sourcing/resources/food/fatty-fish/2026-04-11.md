## Claims Reviewed

Post claims:
- Harvard study: 1-2 servings/week of fatty fish cuts risk of dying from heart disease by 36%
- JAMA study: people with high omega-3s in blood had 27% lower risk of dying early
- Omega-3s (DHA/EPA) protect brain, slow memory decline, reduce dementia risk
- Anti-inflammatory effects reduce chronic disease risk
- Low mercury content in best choices: wild salmon, sardines, anchovies, herring, Arctic char
- Omega-3 index is a predictor of longevity

Post does not include direct study URLs. The JAMA study referenced likely corresponds to published work on the omega-3 index and mortality (e.g., Harris et al.). The Harvard claim aligns with AHA recommendations for fish consumption.
