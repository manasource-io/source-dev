## Claims Reviewed

Buckwheat's general reputation as an antioxidant-rich grain was verified and expanded. The original stub accurately described rutin content and low glycemic index. Verified that:

- The systematic review of 13 RCTs (PMC5986499) confirms meaningful reductions in blood glucose (−0.85 mmol/L), total cholesterol (−0.50 mmol/L), and triglycerides (−0.25 mmol/L)
- Buckwheat is genuinely the only food crop with high natural rutin concentrations (not just one of many)
- ACE-inhibiting peptides are a real documented mechanism for blood pressure effects
- Anti-inflammatory effects via IL-6 and TNF suppression are supported in cell/animal literature; not yet confirmed in large human trials

Credibility set to `moderate` (not `strong`) because most human studies are small, short-term, and mechanistic. The CVD risk marker meta-analysis is solid but the authors themselves call for "large, well-powered, long-term" studies.
