## Claims Reviewed

The Harvard HSPH nutrition page (`nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu/food-features/buckwheat/`) was listed in the frontmatter but had been confirmed as 404 Not Found in the April 2026 source-check. It has been removed.

The body text cited a 13-RCT meta-analysis (PMC5986499) showing significant glucose reduction of 0.85 mmol/L and total cholesterol reduction of 0.50 mmol/L. A newer 2022 meta-analysis of 16 trials (PMC9784502) found smaller, largely non-significant effects (glucose: 0.18 mmol/L, cholesterol: 0.14 mmol/L) and noted considerable heterogeneity. Both references are now included and the body reflects both.

Section headings were using lowercase ID format (e.g. `## blood-sugar-control`) instead of title-case with anchors.

## Notes

The `credibility: moderate` and `overallScore: 5` remain appropriate given the conflicting meta-analyses. The antioxidant/rutin evidence and mechanistic data are well-supported. The practical case for buckwheat as a whole grain replacement (lower GI than refined grains) holds even if the meta-analytic effect sizes are modest.
