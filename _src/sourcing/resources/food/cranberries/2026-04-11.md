# Cranberries — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/cranberries.md`
- **Leads**: Split from `food/red-berries` per research workflow improvement pass.

## Claims Reviewed

Resource created as a split from the consolidated `red-berries.md` entry. The UTI-prevention mechanism (A-type proanthocyanidins) is unique to cranberries and warrants a dedicated resource.

References validated:
- PMC11635990: 2024 meta-analysis and systematic review of cranberry PACs and UTI prevention — confirmed loading; 10 RCTs showing 18% risk reduction at 36mg PACs/day.
- PMC3068482: Berry cardiovascular health review — confirmed applicable for secondary cardiovascular benefit.

## Changes Made

- Created new `cranberries.md` resource file with full frontmatter.
- UTI prevention placed as primary benefit given the uniqueness and strength of evidence.
- Warfarin interaction noted in caveats (high-dose supplements may interact).

## Notes

- The UTI benefit is specific to A-type PACs, not found in most other berries. The distinction between unsweetened juice/extract vs cranberry cocktail (low PACs, high sugar) is clinically important.
- `benefitLevel: 6` reflects that the benefit (UTI prevention) is quite specific and not as broadly applicable as cardiovascular or cognitive benefits in other resources.
