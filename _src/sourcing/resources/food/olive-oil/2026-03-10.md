## Claims Reviewed

All claims in the existing body text are well-supported. The main issue was one low-quality reference (EurekAlert press release) mixed in with otherwise strong sources.

References evaluated:

- **PMID 38709531** (JAMA Network Open 2024, Harvard): `valid/strong` — Flagship prospective cohort study, 92,383 participants, 28 years, demonstrating 28% reduction in dementia-related mortality with >7g/day olive oil. Gold-standard evidence.
- **NIA summary** (nia.nih.gov): `valid/moderate` — Official NIH government summary of the above study. Secondary source but authoritative.
- **EurekAlert press release**: `removed/anecdotal` — Press release for a mouse study; not a primary source. The underlying study (Aging Cell, DOI 10.1111/acel.13076) is valid peer-reviewed research but the EurekAlert URL is not an appropriate reference.
- **MDPI Nutrients RCT**: `valid/moderate` — RCT showing EVOO enhances blood-brain barrier function in mild cognitive impairment. Peer-reviewed.
- **PMC6825477**: `valid/moderate` — Review on EVOO and cognitive decline, oleuropein mechanism. Peer-reviewed.
