## Claims Reviewed

All five PubMed references re-verified:

- PMID 34444859: Valid — Nutrients 2021, ~93k US adults, 20-year follow-up; walnuts 5+/week associated with 14% lower total mortality and ~1.3 extra years life expectancy at age 60.
- PMID 39551942: Valid — Age & Ageing 2024, ASPREE cohort, n=9,916 adults 70+; daily nut consumption associated with 23% lower disability-free survival endpoint risk.
- PMID 31409026: Valid — Antioxidants 2019 review of tree nuts and peanuts in adults 55+; covers cardiovascular, cancer, cognitive outcomes.
- PMID 36839359: Valid — Nutrients 2023 narrative review, nut consumption and cognition; evidence preliminary; walnuts most promising.
- Harvard Health blog: Valid — loads; credible consumer health source summarizing the NEJM 2013 prospective study.
- inc.nutfruit.org/studies-categories/aging/: Valid — loads; international nut industry group; biased source but links to legitimate peer-reviewed studies.

Duplicate reference identified and removed: `https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/15/4/1000` is the identical article as PMID 36839359 (Nishi et al. 2023, Nutrients). The mdpi.com URL was removed from the resource references to eliminate the duplicate.

The Reddit post (1lkm11v, June 2025, 798 upvotes) contained no study citations but surfaced a useful community note: almonds are high in oxalates, which contribute to calcium oxalate kidney stone formation, suggesting a practical caveat not previously in the resource.

## Changes Made
