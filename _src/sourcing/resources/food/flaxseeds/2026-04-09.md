# Flaxseeds Research Session — 2026-04-09

## What Was Found

### New Sources Added

1. **GeroScience 2025 review** (PMC12181604) — A comprehensive 2025 review covering multiple meta-analyses. Key findings: flaxseed reduces SBP by 2–15 mmHg, DBP by 1–7 mmHg, lowers LDL, triglycerides, fasting glucose, and inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6). Also discusses longevity mechanisms (SIRT1, autophagy, DNA repair). Strongest effects at 30 g/day for 12+ weeks. This is the highest-quality recent reference found.

2. **PubMed 40365516 — 2025 meta-analysis of 18 RCTs** — Focused specifically on blood pressure outcomes in people with cardiovascular risk factors. Quantified effect sizes: SBP -4.75 mmHg (p ≤ 0.001), DBP -3.09 mmHg (p ≤ 0.001). Notes significant heterogeneity across trials; calls for further high-quality RCTs.

3. **PMC11945857 — 2025 comprehensive review** (PMID 40142110) — Broad secondary review covering cancer, fiber, and cardiovascular data. Notable: cites an RCT showing 25 g/day of ground flaxseed reduced Ki-67 tumor proliferation marker in breast tissue after ~1 month. Also references FLAX-PAD trial: 30 g/day reduced SBP by 10 mmHg and DBP by 7 mmHg over 1 year in PAD patients.

## Open Questions

1. **Cancer RCT status**: The Ki-67 breast tissue trial is promising but small and short (~1 month). No large phase III RCT appears to exist. Worth re-checking in 2027 when more data may be available.
2. **SDG dose standardization**: Different products have variable lignan content. No standard exists for how much SDG a "tablespoon of ground flaxseed" actually delivers — this matters for cancer prevention claims.
3. **ALA conversion variability**: Conversion efficiency to EPA/DHA varies significantly by individual (genetics, dietary fat context, health status). The 5–10% figure is a population average; some individuals convert substantially less.
4. **Interaction with hormone therapies**: Because lignans have weak estrogenic and antiestrogenic activity, their interaction with HRT, tamoxifen, or other hormone-sensitive medications deserves a note in future UI guidance.
