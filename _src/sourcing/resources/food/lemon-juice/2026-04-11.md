## Claims Reviewed

Verified the single reference: `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4265710/`.

- URL loads correctly; confirmed this is a Korean Journal of Urology review of dietary and medical management of nephrolithiasis.
- Confirms: 4oz/day of lemon juice significantly raises urinary citrate levels; compares to pharmacological potassium citrate therapy.
- Additional nuance not yet in resource: the same article notes that high-dose supplemental vitamin C increases urine oxalate, which could worsen some stone types. The resource discusses culinary use which is safe, but this is worth noting for anyone supplementing vitamin C alongside lemon juice for kidney stone prevention.

No incorrect citations or misrepresented claims found.
