## Claims Reviewed

The original stub contained one inaccurate claim requiring correction: the "alkalizing effect once metabolized" statement. While citrate does raise urinary pH and citrate excretion (relevant to kidney stone prevention), the body maintains blood pH within 7.35–7.45 regardless of diet. The claim as stated implies a systemic body-alkalizing effect, which is not supported and has pseudoscientific connotations. Updated body clarifies the distinction.

**Reference removed**: MedicalNewsToday editorial (no primary research citations; editorial/consumer content). Replaced with PMC4265710 (Korean J Urology, kidney stone prevention via citrate therapy — directly supports the citric acid/citrate benefit).

The vitamin C → iron absorption claim is valid and well-supported by established nutritional biochemistry. The immune support claim is valid. The kidney stone prevention claim is exceptionally well-supported.
