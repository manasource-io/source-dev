## Claims Verified

### Cognitive Protection (37% reduction, OR 0.63)
PubMed 39947165 — Karger Neuroepidemiology 2025 meta-analysis (18 studies, 58,929 participants) — fully confirmed. The OR 0.63 (95% CI: 0.54–0.73) directly supports the 37% cognitive impairment reduction claim. Still the strongest available evidence for this claim. No newer competing meta-analysis found.

### Cardiovascular Health
New meta-analysis found: PMC11573487 (Kim & Je, Epidemiology and Health, June 2024) — 38 prospective cohort datasets, 1,956,549 participants. Green tea 3+ times per week associated with 22% lower CVD mortality (RR 0.86, 95% CI: 0.79–0.94). This is a meaningful upgrade to the cardiovascular section, which previously cited only general observational data and a null RCT result in metabolic syndrome patients. Added as a new reference.
