## Claims Reviewed

Resource created as a split from the consolidated `red-berries.md` entry. Raspberries were the primary subject of ellagic acid and fiber claims in the original file.

References validated:
- PMC3068482: Berry cardiovascular health review — confirmed applicable.
- PMC8533526: Anthocyanins and colorectal cancer — confirmed applicable; ellagic acid (converted to urolithins by gut bacteria) is a central mechanism for raspberries specifically.

## Changes Made

- Created new `raspberries.md` resource file with full frontmatter.
- Highlighted urolithin production from ellagic acid as a distinctive feature linking raspberries to mitophagy activation.

## Notes

- The urolithin story is compelling but highly microbiome-dependent — not all individuals produce urolithins equally. Worth noting in future body text updates.
- Raspberries have the highest fiber density of common berries (~6.5g/100g) — a distinctive practical selling point.
