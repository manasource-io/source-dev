## Claims Reviewed

The original resource had two references and a single-sentence description. Both references are high-quality:

- **Examine.com coffee page** — valid, strong. Comprehensive evidence summary covering cognitive, metabolic, and cardiovascular outcomes with links to primary literature.
- **Poole et al. 2017 BMJ** (j5024) — valid, strong. Umbrella review of 201 meta-analyses covering 67 outcomes. Found coffee associated with benefit in the majority of outcomes studied, with greatest benefit at 3–4 cups/day. Commonly cited as the definitive overview of coffee health research.

Claims verified:

- **Alertness**: Caffeine's mechanism as an adenosine receptor antagonist is exceptionally well-established. Dose-response for attention, reaction time, and mood is documented in hundreds of RCTs.
- **Metabolic protection (T2DM)**: 25–30% reduced risk in high consumers; effect seen with decaf too, pointing to chlorogenic acids. Multiple independent meta-analyses confirm.
- **Liver protection**: Association with reduced NAFLD, cirrhosis, and hepatocellular carcinoma is strong and consistent.
- **Cardiovascular**: J-curve relationship — peak benefit at 3–4 cups. Unfiltered coffee raises LDL (diterpenes); filtered does not. BMJ umbrella review confirms reduced CVD risk overall.
- **Neuroprotection**: Parkinson's risk reduction is one of the most replicated diet-disease associations in epidemiology. Alzheimer's association is positive but less consistent.
