## Claims Reviewed

**Critical issue**: The only existing reference — `https://www.reddit.com/r/Biohackers/comments/1e502tj/eggs_are_extremely_neuroprotective/` — is a Reddit community discussion post. This is anecdotal, not a primary source, and cannot be accessed (blocked by browser safety restrictions). Marked as `removed` in source-checks.csv. Replaced with three peer-reviewed references.

Replacement references:

- PubMed 29451849: comprehensive review of eggs, choline, and lutein on cognition across the lifespan
- PMC12538690: 2025 systematic review of egg intake and cognitive function, 38,722 participants
- ScienceDirect (J Nutrition 2024): Alzheimer's dementia risk reduction with egg intake — 47% risk reduction at ≥1 egg/week

Key findings:

- Choline: ~50–70% daily value in 2 eggs; precursor to acetylcholine; accounts for 39% of the Alzheimer's risk reduction effect
- Lutein: principal carotenoid in brain tissue; crosses blood-brain barrier; reduces neuroinflammation and Alzheimer's pathology markers
- Complete protein: high biological value; supports muscle synthesis and tissue repair
- Whole eggs required: yolk contains the neuroprotective micronutrients
