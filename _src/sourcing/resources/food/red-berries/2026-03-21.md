## Claims Reviewed

The existing stub correctly identified anthocyanins as the key phytochemical and cited cardiovascular and cognitive benefits. The single PMC reference (PMC3068482) was verified — it is a strong cardiovascular review covering strawberries, raspberries, and cranberries, confirming LDL oxidation, lipid peroxidation, antioxidant capacity, and glucose metabolism improvements.

**Issue found**: The cognitive claim ("Regular consumption of red berries is linked to improved cognitive function") is not supported by PMC3068482, which explicitly does not address cognition. This was relabeled as "emerging evidence" in the updated body, framed with appropriate hedging.

**Issue found**: The anti-inflammatory claim is partially overstated — the PMC review notes that most clinical studies found no significant effect on circulating inflammatory biomarkers (with one cranberry exception). Updated to reflect this nuance.

Added cranberry proanthocyanidins → urinary tract benefit as a distinct, well-documented claim with its own benefit entry.
