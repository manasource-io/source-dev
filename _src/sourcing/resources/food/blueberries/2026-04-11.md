- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/blueberries.md`

## Claims Reviewed

Resource created as a split from the consolidated `red-berries.md` entry. Blueberries were the primary focus of the cognitive and cancer-prevention claims in the original file.

References validated:
- PMC3068482: Berry cardiovascular health review — confirmed applicable to blueberries as high-anthocyanin fruit.
- PMC8533526: Anthocyanins and colorectal cancer — confirmed applicable; replaces the incorrectly cited PMC8910006 from the original red-berries file.

## Changes Made

- Created new `blueberries.md` resource file with full frontmatter.
- Added `mind-expansion` metric effect in `effects.json` (gain 5) to reflect cognitive protection evidence unique to blueberries.
- `resist-protect` effect now shared across all 4 berry types (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, cranberries) with gain 7.

## Notes

- The cognitive/neuroprotective benefit is "emerging" in clinical trials; strongest evidence is mechanistic and observational. Credibility stays at `moderate`.
- Pterostilbene content is distinctive and noteworthy — no other common berry has this compound at meaningful levels.
