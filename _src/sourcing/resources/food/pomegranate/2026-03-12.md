# Pomegranate — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/pomegranate.md`
- **Leads**: PubMed searches for urolithin A, blood pressure, punicalagins; ScienceDaily coverage of urolithin A discovery

## Claims Reviewed

The original stub correctly identified punicalagins and punicic acid as key bioactives, and correctly noted that antioxidant activity exceeds red wine and green tea. Expanded substantially to include:

- Blood pressure: The 2024 meta-analysis of 22 RCTs is definitive and large enough to be authoritative
- Urolithin A pathway: Well-supported in animal models; 2024 human RCT (PMC10791945) confirms muscle and immune benefits with supplemental UA — dietary pomegranate is a precursor but conversion is microbiome-dependent (~40% of people)
- Healthline reference kept (moderate credibility, RD-reviewed) as a suitable overview source alongside the primary literature

The `credibility: moderate` rating reflects that while the blood pressure evidence is strong, the urolithin A benefit from dietary pomegranate (as opposed to supplemental UA) is more limited due to microbiome variability. The overall benefit is real but depends significantly on the individual's gut bacteria.
