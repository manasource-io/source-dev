# Sweet Potatoes — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/food/sweet-potatoes.md`
- **Leads**: Web search on sweet potato beta-carotene, glycemic index, and antioxidant research; Harvard Nutrition Source

## Claims Reviewed

The original resource claimed sweet potatoes "prevent blood sugar spikes" — this is an oversimplification. Blood sugar impact depends heavily on preparation: boiled sweet potatoes have a low GI (~44), while baked/roasted versions can reach medium-high GI. Updated the body to clarify this nuance.

Beta-carotene content and immune/vision benefits are well-supported. The cancer risk reduction claim (observational studies only) was added with appropriate caveats.

The Harvard URL in the frontmatter now redirects to nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu — updated the reference to the canonical URL.
