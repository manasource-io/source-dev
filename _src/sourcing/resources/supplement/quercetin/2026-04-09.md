# Quercetin — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/supplements/quercetin.md`
- **Leads**: https://desmolysium.com/longevity/ — quercetin at 2 g/day in "hit-and-run" senolytic cycles with dasatinib

## Claims Reviewed

The desmolysium protocol uses quercetin specifically as part of the D+Q senolytic combination — not as a daily standalone supplement. This framing is preserved and emphasized in the resource.

Key references verified:
- **PMID 31542391** (Mayo Clinic, EBioMedicine 2019): First-in-human D+Q trial; 14 patients with diabetic kidney disease; significant reduction in senescent cell markers after 3 days — confirmed pertinent and strong
- **Lancet EBioMedicine 2025** (STAMINA pilot): 12 older adults with MCI/slow gait; intermittent D+Q protocol feasibility confirmed — confirmed pertinent and moderate (early-phase, n=12)
- **PMC12181558** (GeroScience 2025): D+Q chromatin effects in senescent VSMCs — confirmed pertinent, mechanistic support
- **Examine.com/supplements/quercetin/**: Evidence summary, confirmed valid as overview resource

## Notes

The senolytic framing requires careful balance — the mechanism is genuinely exciting and the human trials are the most advanced in the field, but all are early-phase pilot studies. The resource explicitly calls out that the senolytic benefit requires prescription dasatinib + intermittent high-dose protocol, not daily supplementation. This is accurate and important for a consumer health app.
