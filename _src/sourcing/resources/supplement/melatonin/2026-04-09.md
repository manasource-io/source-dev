# Melatonin Source Check — 2026-04-09

https://desmolysium.com/vitality/ — Melatonin mentioned as a sleep aid; discussed but not the author's primary approach. Used as the trigger to research and create a full supplement entry.

## Claims Reviewed

| Claim | Verdict | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Melatonin reduces sleep onset latency | Supported | Meta-analyses show 7–18 min reduction; Cruz-Sanabria 2024 confirms dose-response peaking ~4mg |
| Low doses (0.5–1mg) best for circadian phase shifting | Supported | Well-established in circadian biology literature; high doses can saturate receptors |
| Effective for jet lag and shift work | Supported | Consistent RCT evidence across populations |
| Less effective than CBT-I for chronic primary insomnia | Supported | CBT-I remains first-line; melatonin does not significantly increase total sleep time in healthy adults |
| Safe with no dependency or rebound insomnia | Supported | No established withdrawal or rebound effects in controlled studies |
| Improves cognitive markers in adults with cognitive impairment | Supported (moderate) | 2025 meta-analysis; most relevant for older adults with existing impairment, not healthy populations |

## References Confirmed

- **PubMed 33417003** — Valid, strong. Meta-analysis of RCTs; PSQI improvements and sleep onset latency reductions. Core evidence for sleep-onset benefit.
- **Cruz-Sanabria 2024 (J Pineal Research, doi:10.1111/jpi.12985)** — Valid, strong. Dose-response meta-analysis; 4mg/day identified as approximate peak for sleep quality; timing moderates effect. Core evidence for dosing section.
- **PMC12581400** — Valid, moderate. 2025 meta-analysis on melatonin and cognition in adults with cognitive impairment. Supports cognitive-aging benefit ID; rated moderate because it applies to an impaired population, not healthy adults.
- **examine.com/supplements/melatonin/** — Valid, strong. Comprehensive, regularly updated overview; corroborates all major claims.

## Notes

The cognitive-aging benefit ID was included because the 2025 meta-analysis (PMC12581400) is a meaningful and well-sourced addition, but it is rated moderate credibility because it applies specifically to adults with existing cognitive impairment. Future sessions should monitor whether melatonin's role in healthy aging gains stronger primary evidence.

The dosing table highlights the counterintuitive finding that lower doses (0.5–1mg) are preferred for circadian phase shifting while higher doses (up to 5mg) may be used for sleep onset — a distinction worth emphasizing given that most commercial products are dosed far above what circadian evidence supports.
