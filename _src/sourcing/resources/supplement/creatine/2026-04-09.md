## New Research Found

### PubMed 40971619 — Creatine and Cognition in Aging (Systematic Review, 2025/2026)
- **Study**: Systematic review; 6 studies, 1,542 participants; Nutrition Reviews (Oxford)
- **Findings**: 83% of studies (5 of 6) reported positive creatine-cognition relationship in older adults; memory and attention domains most consistent; methodological quality mixed (1 good, 2 fair, 3 poor); authors call for high-quality RCTs
- **Action**: Added to references and source-checks.csv; described in new Aging and Longevity Applications section

### PubMed 41062952 — Creatine + Exercise in Older Adults (Meta-Analysis, 2025)
- **Study**: Systematic review and meta-analysis; 20 studies, 1,093 older adults; European Review of Aging and Physical Activity
- **Findings**: Creatine + exercise training significantly improved 1RM strength (mean difference +2.12 kg, p=0.001) and reduced body fat percentage (mean difference -0.55%, p=0.026); no significant effect on bone mineral density
- **Action**: Added to references and source-checks.csv; supports aging muscle and body composition claims in new section

### PubMed 40673730 — Creatine for Older Adults and Clinical Populations (Narrative Review, 2025)
- **Study**: Narrative review; Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition
- **Findings**: CrM safe for older adults; documented benefits across lean body mass, muscle strength, functional ability (sit-to-stand), glucose kinetics, cognition, and memory; potential application for sarcopenia, osteoporosis, frailty, and metabolic/neuromuscular disorders; notes upper-body strength benefits more robust than lower-body
- **Action**: Added to references and source-checks.csv; supports the full scope of the new Aging section

## Additional Findings (Not Added as References)

- **CABA Trial (Alzheimer's pilot, PubMed 40395689)**: 19 participants, 8 weeks at 20 g/day CrM; brain creatine +11%; significant cognitive improvements on global and fluid composites.
