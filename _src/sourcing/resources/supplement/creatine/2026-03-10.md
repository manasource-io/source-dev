## Claims Reviewed

The original resource had only a single sentence: "Creatine supplementation significantly enhances muscle strength, supports cognitive function, and reduces mental fatigue." This is directionally accurate but lacked nuance and supporting structure.

Claims verified:

- **Strength and power**: Extensively supported by meta-analyses. 5–15% strength gains over placebo in RCTs. Very well-established.
- **Muscle recovery**: Supported but effects on muscle damage markers are mixed. Primarily works through ATP replenishment enabling more training volume.
- **Cognitive support**: Systematic review of 6 RCTs shows improvement in short-term memory and intelligence/reasoning, with greatest effects in vegetarians and under metabolic stress (sleep deprivation, aging). Not universally effective across all cognitive domains.
- **Dosing**: 3–5 g/day standard; loading phase optional. Monohydrate is the most evidence-backed form.
- **Safety**: Well-established safety profile. Kidney concerns in healthy adults are not substantiated by evidence.
