## Claims Reviewed

The original resource cited a Reddit post as a reference — this was removed. The existing PubMed 23377209 is a meta-analysis on vitamin D and depression (not mortality), and is valid for the mood benefit claim. The original resource's mortality claim ("7% reduction, 18 RCTs, 57,311 participants") is superseded by a more comprehensive 2023 meta-analysis of 80 RCTs with 163,000+ participants.

The C. elegans lifespan claim in the body is accurate (SKN-1, IRE-1, XBP-1 pathways) but lacks a direct reference. This should be sourced to the underlying animal study in a future session.

Key finding: the most robust evidence is for all-cause mortality reduction (OR 0.95 from 80-RCT meta-analysis), bone health, and immune function. Mood benefit evidence is associative but real (31k+ participant meta-analysis). The resource is `strong` credibility overall.
