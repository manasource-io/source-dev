# Taurine — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/supplements/taurine.md`
- **Leads**: NIH news release and Fortune.com article on 2025 Science paper challenging taurine decline hypothesis

## Claims Reviewed

The resource description stated taurine "declines with aging" — a claim sourced from the 2023 Singh et al. *Science* paper that used cross-sectional comparisons. A 2025 *Science* paper (Fernandez et al., PMID 40472098) from NIH used **longitudinal** data from the Baltimore Longitudinal Study of Aging and found that taurine levels actually **increase or remain unchanged** with age when tracking the same individuals over time. This directly contradicts the cross-sectional finding and substantially undermines the "taurine deficiency as aging driver" hypothesis.

A separate 2025 *Aging Cell* paper (PMC12507425) was already referenced in the resource and makes a complementary argument that taurine decline may be a biomarker of diet/lifestyle changes rather than a causal driver.
