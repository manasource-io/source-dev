# TMG (Trimethylglycine) — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/supplements/tmg.md`
- **Leads**: https://desmolysium.com/longevity/ — TMG at 1 g/day for methylation support

## Claims Reviewed

TMG's primary uses in the longevity context are homocysteine lowering and methylation support. The evidence for homocysteine lowering is solid (2013 meta-analysis). The translation to hard cardiovascular outcomes is uncertain — an important nuance preserved in the resource.

Key references verified:
- **PMC8224793** (2021 Betaine comprehensive review): Covers homocysteine, liver health, osmolyte function, NAFLD — valid and pertinent across all three benefit IDs
- **PMID 23230003** (2013 RCT meta-analysis): Directly validates the homocysteine-reduction claim — strong evidence
- **Examine.com/supplements/betaine/**: Valid overview

## Notes

The high-dose LDL safety note (6 g+/day) is important to flag — it potentially offsets cardiovascular benefit from homocysteine reduction. The 1 g/day dose used on desmolysium.com is well within the safe range and consistent with methylation/liver support applications. The homocysteine-hard-outcomes disconnect is a genuine nuance that mirrors the B-vitamin homocysteine lowering trial data.
