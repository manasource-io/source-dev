# Astaxanthin — Research Notes

- **Resource**: `resources/nutrition/supplements/astaxanthin.md`
- **Leads**: https://desmolysium.com/longevity/ — described as "only proven longevity supplement" based on ITP animal data

## Claims Reviewed

The author's "only proven longevity supplement" claim refers specifically to the 2024 ITP Geroscience study (PMID 38041783) — the most rigorous animal lifespan platform available. This is a notable result (first carotenoid to achieve a significant ITP result), but the framing requires careful contextualization: the effect was male-only in this study, and no human lifespan data exists.

Key references verified:
- **PMID 38041783**: ITP 2024 study — 12% male median lifespan extension, multi-site replication, p=0.003. Directly supports longevity-lifespan claim. Strong evidence tier for animal data.
- **PMC7401246**: PMC geroprotector review — covers FOXO3, antioxidant mechanism, brain penetration. Valid as mechanistic support.
- **PMID 33946587**: 2021 Nutrients meta-analysis (9 RCTs, skin outcomes) — directly supports skin-health benefit. Strong human RCT tier.
- **Examine.com**: Valid evidence summary.

## Notes

The ITP result is genuinely exciting and distinguishes astaxanthin from most supplements with only in vitro or weak clinical data. However, the sex-specific effect (males only in this study) warrants monitoring — it is a common pattern in aging models but the mechanism is unknown here. No human longevity RCTs are in progress as of 2026. The skin evidence provides a grounded, validated benefit for the consumer app context.
