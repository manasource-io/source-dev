## File Created

`resources/nutrition/supplements/whey-protein.md` — new supplement resource covering:
- What whey protein is and its three forms (concentrate, isolate, hydrolysate)
- Leucine threshold and AKT/mTOR signaling mechanism
- MPS evidence (muscle-protein-synthesis section)
- Sarcopenia prevention considerations for older adults including anabolic resistance and dosing guidance (sarcopenia-prevention section)
- Satiety and body composition via GLP-1/CCK stimulation (satiety section)
- Dosing (20–40 g per serving, distributed across meals)
- Limitations: no advantage over matched-dose complete proteins; modest cardiometabolic effects
- Contraindications: milk allergy, kidney disease caution

## Notes

The older adult meta-analysis (39303495) usefully anchors the caveat that whey alone without exercise shows limited effects — this nuance is reflected in the sarcopenia-prevention section. The 2025 PMC review's insulin resistance finding at high doses is noted under limitations. No dose-response concerns for healthy adults at standard 20–40 g servings. Future session could look for a direct head-to-head comparison RCT (whey vs. plant protein at matched leucine doses) if that comparison becomes a focus.
