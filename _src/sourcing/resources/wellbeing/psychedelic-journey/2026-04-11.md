## Claims Reviewed

Re-verified all three existing references. Found two critically wrong references:

1. `https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-022-02161-z` — Previously logged as "psilocybin vs escitalopram RCT in Nature Medicine." WebSearch cannot confirm this DOI is a psilocybin study; search results for this DOI return unrelated cancer and immunology articles. The confirmed psilocybin vs escitalopram trial (Carhart-Harris et al. 2021) was published in NEJM with DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2032994, not in Nature Medicine. This URL has been removed.

2. `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30392758/` — Previously logged as "Davis et al. 2018 psilocybin-assisted therapy for MDD." When fetched, PMID 30392758 is "Epidemiology and Overview of Gliomas" in Seminars in Oncology Nursing (2018). Completely unrelated to psilocybin. Removed.

3. `https://www.nature.com/articles/s41514-025-00244-x` — Confirmed valid (PMID 40628762, npj Aging 2025).

The Reddit post (1mcrt2d, July 2025, 460 upvotes) confirms the npj Aging study is already captured. Comment noted the in vitro psilocin concentration (10 microM) may not translate to human dosing — incorporated as a caveat.

Replacement references added:
- PMID 33146667: Davis et al. JAMA Psychiatry 2021 — n=27 RCT, psilocybin for MDD, 71% clinical response at week 4, large effect sizes
- PMID 27909165: Griffiths et al. J Psychopharmacol 2016 — n=51 cancer patients, sustained decreases in depression/anxiety, 80% response at 6-month follow-up (Hopkins)
- PMID 27909164: Ross et al. J Psychopharmacol 2016 — n=29 cancer patients, rapid and sustained symptom reduction, 80% response at 6+ months (NYU companion trial)
